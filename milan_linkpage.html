Milan

Milan, a metropolis in Italy's northern Lombardy region, is a global capital of fashion and design. Home to the national stock exchange, it’s a financial hub also known for its high-end restaurants and shops.

Naviglio Grande

Milan is also known for its canals. It is very pretty area, especially, reaching its peak at night. There are plenty of restaurants, places to drink and listen to music. It is a very cool area. The two Navigli (the Great and the Pavese) are two of the most fashionable areas of the city. Sites usually frequented by young hipsters increasingly, looking to take a shot in the thousands of terraces that are located on both sides of the channels, for a walk around the neighborhood, play an instrument ... is the most bohemian area of Milan, something like the Milanese Soho. Lately is full of places to take the "appetizer", a new concept in northern Italy. "The appetizer" is have a drink at one of these places, and the price of the cup, can accompany an aperitif in the form of food: pizza, pasta, rice, sandwiches, fruit, sweets ... In short, looking at it realistically, a free buffet with glass gift. There are many venues in Milan offering snacks at certain times, and prices will rotate, according to local and area, usually between 7 € and 10 €. Great price for dinner in Milan, because that's just what you normally costs a drink on a terrace set medium well. In my view, this unmissable part of Milan remains the most authentic of the city, the more traditional with its village atmosphere with its bistros, trattorias, the channel passes short, a little corner of paradise. Landmark creators, designers, artists and musicians, this area is popular with young avant-garde Milanese, a bit bohemian and hipster! Discover the timeless day losing yourself in the streets, strolling along the canal on each side. In the evening the area is very lively with many bars restaurants filled, and the party continues in the street, all the joy and good humor
